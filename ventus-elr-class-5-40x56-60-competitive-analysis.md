# Ventus ELR Class — Competitive Analysis

**Class:** Extreme Long Range FFP, 5-40× / 6-36× / 7-35× magnification, 56/60mm objective, **34mm tube**
**Ventus SKUs in class:** 6 (FFP 5-40×56, 6-36×56, 7-35×56, 5-40×60, 6-36×60, 7-35×60)
**Related RikaNV model:** GML 5-40×60 FFP (flagship)

> **Tube-strict comparison.** Ventus is 34mm in this class. ZCO ZC527 and Tangent Theta TT735P both use **36mm tubes** — physically 2mm larger, structurally different internal geometry. They are excluded from direct comparison below; see exclusion notes in Section 7.

---

## 1. TL;DR — Main Findings

1. **Within 34mm tube class**, Ventus elevation 37.8 MIL is second behind March-FX (40 MIL) and ahead of everyone else — Nightforce ATACR 7-35 (29 MIL), Schmidt & Bender PM II 5-45 HP (27 MIL), Vortex Razor Gen III 6-36 (36.1 MRAD), Vector Continental (26 MIL).
2. **Windage 20.4 MIL mid-pack within 34mm.** Better than S&B HP (12 MIL) and March (14 MIL), on par with Vortex Gen III (15.5 MIL), below Nightforce ATACR (18 MIL).
3. **Weight 940g is class-leading among 34mm premium ELR optics.** Only Vector Continental (810g) is lighter. March-FX 1015g, NF ATACR 1113g, S&B 1130g, Vortex Razor III 1278g.
4. **Length 393mm is class-shortest.** NF ATACR 406mm, March-FX 410mm, S&B 434mm, Vortex Razor Gen III 387mm (close), Vector 392mm (tied).
5. **FOV rankings inconclusive without lab verification.** Ventus published figures match Vector Continental but need independent measurement against alpha-tier.
6. **Price class: Ventus/Vector sub-$1000 vs NF/S&B/March $3,000–$4,700.** Realistic retail peer is Vector Continental. Against alpha-tier, positioning is value-framed.

## 6. Competitive Position Summary (34mm-strict)

### Where Ventus wins (on paper)

- **Elevation travel** — 37.8 MIL is 2nd in 34mm class behind only March-FX; class-leading among sub-$3K 34mm optics.
- **Windage travel** — 20.4 MIL is generous; better than most 34mm European premium.
- **Weight** — lightest 34mm FFP at this zoom range except Vector.
- **Length** — tied for shortest, meaningful for rifle balance.
- **Click options** — both MOA (0.25) and MIL (0.1) available.
- **Price class** — OEM/Chinese tier, structural cost advantage over Western premium.

### Where Ventus has risk / uncertainty

- **Optical quality** is not visible from specs. Glass, coatings, chromatic aberration — these are what $3k–$5k buys.
- **Tracking / return-to-zero / repeatability** — the mechanical reliability gap that makes premium premium. Claims of "1000G shockproof" and "IP67" are standard spec-sheet items.
- **FOV numbers** — manufacturer-published, not independently measured.
- **No published MTBF or warranty data.**

### Realistic competitive peer

Ventus's realistic peer at retail is **Vector Optics Continental 5-30×56 FFP** (~$833). Against Vector, Ventus beats elevation (37.8 vs 26 MIL) and matches form factor.

Against 34mm premium (NF ATACR, S&B PM II HP, March-FX, Vortex Razor III), the positioning argument is "85% of the travel, 75% of the weight savings, at 25% of the price" — with the caveat that the remaining 15–25% (glass, mechanical repeatability) is exactly what separates $1k from $5k.
