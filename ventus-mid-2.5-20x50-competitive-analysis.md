# Ventus Mid-Range 2.5-20× / 2.5-15× / 3-18× × 50mm Class — Competitive Analysis

**Class:** Mid-to-long range FFP/SFP, 2.5-20× / 2.5-15× / 3-18× magnification, 50mm objective, 30mm tube
**Ventus SKUs in class:** 6 (FFP 2.5-20×50, FFP 2.5-15×50, FFP 3-18×50, SFP 2.5-20×50, SFP 2.5-15×50, SFP 3-18×50)

---

## 1. TL;DR — Main Findings

1. **Elevation travel (37.8 MIL) on a 30mm tube is class-leading among 30mm-tube 2.5-20/3-18 FFP options.** Only Vector Continental 3-18×50 FFP (44 MIL, 34mm tube) exceeds it. Ventus beats Athlon Ares ETR (32 MIL 34mm), Leupold Mark 5HD (29 MIL 35mm), Burris XTR III (35 MIL 34mm).
2. **Windage travel (32 MIL) is the highest in this class across any tube size.** Double Vortex PST Gen II (11 MIL) and Arken SH-4 (14 MIL), 25–30% more than Athlon Ares ETR (25 MIL) and Leupold Mark 5HD (23 MIL).
3. **2.5× low-end magnification on a 50mm objective is uncontested** among major Western brands. Vortex starts at 3×, Athlon at 3× or 4×, Leupold at 3.6×, Burris at 3.3×. Ventus 2.5-20× and 2.5-15× are unique combinations.
4. **6 SKU family shares identical mechanics** — differs only by zoom range and focal plane. Clean product design.
5. **Turret package is premium-tier:** locking + zero stop + side focus 10yds–∞ + dual click (0.1 MIL / ¼ MOA).
6. **Realistic competitive peer is Vector Continental 3-18×50 FFP (~$524–799).** Against Vector, Ventus trades 34mm tube (larger) for 2.5× low-end magnification and higher windage travel.

## 6. Competitive Position Summary

### Where Ventus wins (on paper)

- **Elevation travel on a 30mm tube** — 37.8 MIL beats every competitor except the 34mm Vector Continental.
- **Windage travel** — 32 MIL is class-leading by a wide margin.
- **Zoom range (2.5× low-end)** — uncontested among Western brands at 50mm objective.
- **Six-SKU family** — gives buyers choice of 6× or 8× zoom and FP selection at the same mechanical base.
- **Click flexibility** — 0.1 MIL or 1/4 MOA dual option.
- **Turret package** — locking + zero stop + side focus 10yds is premium-tier.
- **Price class** — sub-$900 OEM vs $1,700–$2,200 Leupold / $1,200–$1,700 Burris.

### Where Ventus has risk / uncertainty

- **Tube diameter (30mm)** — at this magnification range, 34mm is the professional standard. PRS-leaning buyers may discount the scope on tube spec alone despite the travel numbers.
- **Optical quality** — no public reviews of Jupiter/Venus 50mm glass. Glass, coatings, CA, edge sharpness all unknown.
- **Tracking repeatability** — 37.8 MIL on a 30mm tube is aggressive; worth verification.
- **3-18× FFP weight at 785g** vs Leupold Mark 5HD 737g (different objective/tube spec) — close but Leupold keeps the edge with a 35mm tube and premium glass.
- **No published MTBF or warranty data.**

### Realistic competitive peer

**Vector Optics Continental 3-18×50 FFP (SCFF-28, ~$524–799)** is the closest retail peer. Against Vector:
- Vector wins on tube diameter (34mm) and elevation ceiling (44 MIL vs 37.8 MIL).
- Ventus wins on zoom range (2.5-20× available), windage (32 vs 16 MIL), and weight on 3-18× variant (785g vs 820g).
- Both use 0.1 MIL clicks.

**Burris XTR III 3.3-18×50 ($1,199–1,700)** is the next-tier USA competitor. Against it:
- Ventus wins on elevation (37.8 vs 35 MIL), windage (32 vs 16 MIL), and dual click option.
- Burris wins on proven tracking, glass reputation, and US warranty/service.

**Leupold Mark 5HD 3.6-18×44 ($1,700–2,200)** is the USA premium reference. Ventus wins on elevation, windage, price, and zoom range; Leupold wins on build quality, optical reputation, and resale.
