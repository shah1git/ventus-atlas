# Ventus Mid-Range 2.5-20× / 2.5-15× / 3-18× × 50mm Class — Competitive Analysis

**Class:** Mid-to-long range FFP/SFP, 2.5-20× / 2.5-15× / 3-18× magnification, 50mm objective, **30mm tube**
**Ventus SKUs in class:** 6 (three zoom ranges × FFP and SFP each)

> **Tube-strict comparison.** Ventus is 30mm in this class. This tier is unusual — most Western FFP 3-18×50 and 5-25×50 scopes use 34mm or 35mm tubes to achieve the travel needed at these magnifications. 30mm-tube peers are scarce. Non-30mm competitors (Vector Continental 34mm, Athlon Ares ETR 34mm, Arken SH-4 34mm, Leupold Mark 5HD 35mm, Bushnell Match Pro ED 34mm, Burris XTR III 34mm) are excluded; they appear in 34mm-tube analyses.

---

## 1. TL;DR — Main Findings

1. **Ventus is nearly alone in 30mm-tube 50mm-objective FFP at 3-18×.** The only direct 30mm-tube peer in this magnification range is Vortex Viper PST Gen II 3-15×44 FFP (different objective — 44mm). No other mainstream Western brand offers a 30mm-tube 50mm-objective FFP in this zoom range.
2. **Ventus 37.8 MIL elevation far exceeds the only 30mm tube peer** (Vortex PST at 22 MIL). Against the structurally-similar 30mm but different-objective Vortex, Ventus offers 72% more elevation.
3. **32 MIL windage is roughly 3× Vortex PST's 11 MIL.** Consistent pattern with elevation — Ventus's 30mm-tube internals deliver exceptional travel figures.
4. **2.5× low-end magnification on a 50mm objective is uncontested** across all tube diameters and brands for this class.
5. **6 SKU family shares identical mechanics** — zoom range (2.5-15, 2.5-20, 3-18) and focal plane are the only variables.
6. **Turret package complete:** 0.1 MIL / 1/4 MOA dual click, locking + zero stop, side focus 10yds–∞.

## 6. Competitive Position Summary

### Where Ventus wins (tube-strict, on paper)

- **Elevation** — 37.8 MIL is 72% more than only 30mm peer.
- **Windage** — 32 MIL is ~3× only 30mm peer.
- **Zoom range** — 2.5× low-end is uncontested; 20× top-end beats Vortex PST's 15×.
- **6-SKU family** gives buyer flexibility within the tube class.
- **Turret spec** — dual click option is atypical in this tier.
- **Price class** — sub-$900 OEM vs $999–1,199 Vortex PST.

### Where Ventus has risk / uncertainty

- **37.8 MIL on 30mm tube** — at the physical ceiling; tracking verification recommended.
- **Weight** — 785g for 3-18× is good but Vortex PST (797g) is a close peer; the 840g 2.5-20/2.5-15 SKUs are heavier.
- **Near-solitary 30mm 50mm FFP position** — which is both a differentiator and a warning sign. Buyers expecting 34mm for this magnification range may discount the scope on tube spec alone.
- **Optical quality** — no public reviews; Jupiter/Venus 50mm glass unverified at 20× magnification.
- **No published MTBF or warranty data.**

### Realistic retail peer

**Vortex Viper PST Gen II 3-15×44 FFP ($999–1,199)** is the only direct 30mm-tube FFP peer. Against PST:
- Ventus wins elevation (37.8 vs 22 MIL), windage (32 vs 11 MIL), zoom range (2.5-20× vs 3-15×), click flexibility (dual vs single), and 50mm vs 44mm objective.
- PST wins on proven tracking track record, US warranty (VIP lifetime), and reticle variety.

For buyers willing to go 34mm tube, competitors expand dramatically (Vector Continental 3-18×50, Burris XTR III, Bushnell Match Pro ED, Athlon Ares ETR, Leupold Mark 5HD). But that's a different tube class — see 34mm-specific analyses.
