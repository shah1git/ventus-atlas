# Ventus LPVO 1-8× Class — Competitive Analysis

**Class:** Low-Power Variable Optic, 1-8× magnification, 24/26/28mm objective, 30mm or 34mm tube
**Ventus SKUs in class:** 4 (FFP 1-8×26, FFP 1-8×24, SFP 1-8×28, SFP 1-8×24)

> **Tube-strict comparison.** Tube diameter is structurally fixed and determines internal erector travel room — 30mm and 34mm are different engineering classes. This analysis compares Ventus SKUs only against competitors **on the same tube**. Ventus offers both tube sizes in this class, so the analysis is split into a **30mm sub-class** and a **34mm sub-class**.

---

## 1. TL;DR — Main Findings

1. **Within the 30mm tube class**, Ventus's 34.9 MIL total elevation beats Vector Continental's 40 MIL headline *only with tube-strict sorting removed* — because Vector's 40 MIL is its SFP 1-8×24 on a 30mm tube. At 30mm: Ventus FFP 34.9 MIL vs Vector SFP 40 MIL vs Primary Arms PLxC 29.1 MIL. Vector leads on elevation; Ventus sits mid.
2. **Within the 34mm tube class**, Ventus's 34.9 MIL matches Trijicon VCOG (35 MIL) and beats Vortex Razor Gen III (30 MIL), Burris XTR II (30 MIL), Trijicon Credo (29 MIL), Athlon Ares ETR (29.1 MIL).
3. **Weight on 30mm Ventus SKUs (525g / 551g)** is lightest among 30mm FFP 1-8× with ≥30 MIL elevation.
4. **1/2 MOA click remains the class-wide weakness** for Ventus across both tube sizes — every competitor uses 0.1 MIL or 1/4 MOA.
5. **Published FOV at 1× (42.7–49.9m / 100m)** exceeds every direct peer, including Chinese-OEM Vector Continental. Worth independent verification.
6. **Realistic retail peer by tube:** 30mm — Vector Continental x8 1-8×24 SFP (~$350–499). 34mm — Trijicon Credo 1-8×28 FFP (~$1,400).

## 6. Competitive Position Summary

### Where Ventus wins (on paper, tube-strict)

- **34mm class:** Ventus FFP 1-8×26 elevation (34.9 MIL) ties Trijicon VCOG at the top; beats Vortex Razor Gen III, Athlon Ares ETR, Trijicon Credo, and discontinued Burris XTR II.
- **34mm class:** Weight (608g) is the lightest FFP 1-8× on 34mm except Athlon Ares ETR (544g, 1-10× substitute).
- **30mm class:** Weight (525g / 551g) is lightest FFP 1-8× with ≥30 MIL elevation.
- **Both classes:** FOV @ 1× is largest published.

### Where Ventus loses / carries risk

- **30mm class:** Vector Continental has higher elevation (40 vs 34.9 MIL) and lower price.
- **Both classes:** 1/2 MOA click is coarsest in class — every other scope uses 0.1 MIL or 1/4 MOA.
- **SFP 1-8×28 (34mm):** 26.2 MIL elevation is lowest in the 34mm comparison.
- **FOV claim** at 1× exceeds even Chinese-OEM Vector Continental — recommend independent lab verification.
- **No public optical-performance reviews** or MTBF/warranty data.

### Realistic retail peers

- **30mm sub-class:** Vector Continental x8 1-8×24 SFP ED ($349–499) is the direct peer. Ventus loses on elevation and click; wins on FFP availability.
- **34mm sub-class:** Trijicon Credo 1-8×28 FFP ($1,389–1,410) is the nearest retail peer on price-capability. Ventus FFP 1-8×26 beats it on elevation (34.9 vs 29 MIL) and weight (608 vs 726g); Credo wins on click precision and glass reputation.
