# Ventus Long-Range 3-24× / 4-24× × 50mm Class — Competitive Analysis

**Class:** Long-range FFP/SFP, 3-24× / 4-24× magnification, 50mm objective, 30mm tube
**Ventus SKUs in class:** 4 (FFP 3-24×50, FFP 4-24×50, SFP 3-24×50, SFP 4-24×50)

---

## 1. TL;DR — Main Findings

1. **Elevation travel (32.0 MIL) leads the 30mm-tube long-range FFP segment.** Beats Nightforce NX8 4-32×50 F1 (26 MRAD, 30mm) and Vortex Viper PST Gen II 5-25×50 (20 MRAD, 30mm) — the only other serious 30mm-tube 50mm-objective FFP long-range optics. Ties Arken SH-4 Gen II (34mm) and Athlon Cronus BTR Gen II (34mm).
2. **Windage travel 26.2 MIL beats every competitor except Nightforce NX8.** S&B PM II 3-20×50, Athlon Cronus, Arken SH-4, Burris XTR III — all lose to Ventus on windage travel.
3. **No direct competitor matches the 3-24× zoom range on a 30mm tube.** Nightforce jumps from 2.5-20 to 4-32; Vortex Viper PST is 5-25; Vector Continental 4-24 requires 34mm + 56mm objective. Ventus's 3-24× at 30mm/50mm is a standalone position.
4. **Weight is competitive.** Ventus 3-24×50 at 792g is the lightest 24×-class FFP with 30+ MIL elevation. Only NF NX8 (811g) is close; everyone else is 845g (Burris XTR III 5.5-30) to 1375g (Vortex Razor Gen II).
5. **Tube diameter is the key caveat.** Ventus's 30mm tube is unusual for this long-range segment — competitors overwhelmingly use 34mm. If Ventus's 32 MIL elevation on 30mm is real (not marketing), this is a rare packaging win; if marketing, it's a risk.
6. **Realistic competitive peer is Vector Continental 4-24×56 FFP (SCFF-40, ~$599).** Against Vector, Ventus loses on tube (30mm vs 34mm) but gains on windage travel (26.2 vs 16 MIL).

## 6. Competitive Position Summary

### Where Ventus wins (on paper)

- **Elevation on 30mm tube** — 32 MIL ties the best 34mm-tube mid-priced competitors (Arken SH-4, Athlon Cronus) and beats every 30mm-tube FFP long-range optic.
- **Windage travel** — 26.2 MIL is class-leading across all tube sizes.
- **Weight** — 3-24×50 at 792g is the lightest long-range FFP with usable elevation in class.
- **3× low-end zoom** — genuinely unique at this objective and tube size.
- **Dual click option** — 0.1 MIL or 1/4 MOA.
- **Price class** — sub-$1000 OEM vs $2,450 (NF NX8) / $1,700–1,900 (Athlon Cronus) / $4,500+ (S&B).

### Where Ventus has risk / uncertainty

- **30mm tube in a long-range class dominated by 34mm.** PRS buyers default to 34mm for this magnification range. 30mm reads as "mid-range" regardless of actual travel spec.
- **32 MIL on 30mm** — plausible but at the ceiling for the tube format. Worth independent verification.
- **Optical quality** — no public reviews of Jupiter/Venus 50mm long-range glass. CA, edge sharpness, tracking repeatability all unknown at the 24× magnification range.
- **Mount rail compatibility** — 34mm rings are the tactical/PRS standard; 30mm requires buyers to commit to that tube format for rifle inventory.
- **No published MTBF or warranty data.**

### Realistic competitive peer

**Vector Optics Continental 4-24×56 FFP (SCFF-40, $599–829)** is the closest retail peer.
- Vector wins on tube diameter (34mm), objective (56mm), and on paper elevation (≥34 MIL).
- Ventus wins on windage (26.2 vs ≥16 MIL), weight (792g 3-24× vs 841g), and the 3× low-end option Vector doesn't have.

**Nightforce NX8 4-32×50 F1 ($2,450)** is the direct tube-and-objective peer.
- NF wins on build quality, proven tracking, and higher max magnification (32×).
- Ventus wins on elevation (32 vs 26 MIL), windage (26.2 vs 20 MIL), lower zoom start (3× vs 4×).
- NF costs ~3× any expected Ventus retail price.

Against Athlon Cronus BTR Gen II (~$1,700–1,900): same elevation (32 MIL), more windage on Ventus, ~220g lighter, 30mm vs 34mm. Athlon wins on glass reputation and warranty.

Against S&B PM II 3-20×50 Ultra Short ($4,500+): S&B wins on optical quality, reticle selection, mechanical pedigree; Ventus wins on elevation, windage, weight, and cost by 5×+.
