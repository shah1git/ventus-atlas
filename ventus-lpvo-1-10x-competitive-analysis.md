# Ventus LPVO 1-10× Class — Competitive Analysis

**Class:** Low-Power Variable Optic, 1-10× magnification, 24/26/28mm objective, 30mm or 34mm tube
**Ventus SKUs in class:** 4 (FFP 1-10×26, FFP 1-10×24, SFP 1-10×28, SFP 1-10×24) — "Jupiter" series

---

## 1. TL;DR — Main Findings

1. **Elevation travel (34.9 MIL) places Ventus mid-top among current 1-10× FFP options.** Beats Vortex Razor HD Gen III (30 MIL), Athlon Ares ETR Gen2 (29 MIL), Trijicon Credo HX (29 MIL). Loses to Vector Continental x10 1-10×28 (51 MIL) and Primary Arms GLx 1-10×24 (~40 MIL).
2. **Weight on 30mm-tube SKUs (527g / 553g) is class-leading among FFP 1-10× options.** Only PA SLx 1-10×28 (SFP) at 542g and Trijicon Credo HX (601g) come close. Vortex Gen III is 610g, Vector Continental 651g, PA GLx 660g, Athlon 762g.
3. **Published FOV @ 1× (42.7–49.9m / 100m) exceeds every Western competitor** — same flag as LPVO 1-8× class. Vector Continental claims 39.2m, Vortex 38.6m, Athlon 39.1m. Ventus claim needs independent verification.
4. **1/2 MOA click is the persistent weakness.** Every competing 1-10× uses 0.1 MIL or 1/4 MOA. Same issue as LPVO 1-8× class.
5. **SFP 1-10×28 is mechanically different** (26.2 MIL total vs 34.9 MIL in the other three SKUs) — identical pattern to the SFP 1-8×28 in the previous class.
6. **Category is thinning.** Burris and Leupold have no current 1-10× LPVO. The direct FFP 1-10× field is just 6 brands (Ventus, Vector, Vortex, PA GLx, Athlon, Trijicon).

### Click Value (⚠ Ventus weakness)

Same story as in the 1-8× class. Every FFP competitor offers 0.1 MIL or 1/4 MOA. Ventus uses 1/2 MOA across all four SKUs. The only competitor also using 1/2 MOA is the Primary Arms SLx 1-10×28 SFP ($450 budget tier).

### Tube Diameter & Form Factor

- **30mm tube FFP 1-10×:** Ventus FFP 1-10×24 (527g), Ventus SFP 1-10×24 (553g), PA GLx (660g). Only 3 models in this niche.
- **34mm tube FFP 1-10×:** Ventus FFP 1-10×26 / SFP 1-10×28 (608g / 646g), Vector Continental (651g), Vortex Razor Gen III (610g), Athlon Ares ETR (762g), Trijicon Credo HX (601g).

### Zoom Penalty at Max Magnification

At 10× the Ventus FOV values (4.27–6.11m @ 100m) are competitive with Vortex Razor Gen III (3.90m), Athlon (3.90m), Trijicon Credo HX (3.90m) — Ventus's published figures are actually wider at max zoom, which again suggests manufacturer-claim inflation vs measured values.

## 6. Competitive Position Summary

### Where Ventus wins (on paper)

- **Elevation travel** — 34.9 MIL beats Vortex Gen III, Athlon, Trijicon Credo HX; loses to Vector Continental and PA GLx only.
- **Weight (30mm tube SKUs)** — class-leading among FFP 1-10× at 527g / 553g.
- **Tube flexibility** — 30mm and 34mm in both FP. No Western brand offers this range.
- **FOV @ 1×** (if verifiable) — largest published in class.
- **Price class** — OEM/sub-$500 tier vs $680–$2000 Western.

### Where Ventus has risk / uncertainty

- **Click value** — 1/2 MOA still the single clearest weakness vs every current FFP 1-10×.
- **SFP 1-10×28 mechanics** — 26.2 MIL elevation is noticeably less than the other three SKUs; buyers may feel misled if positioning doesn't flag this.
- **FOV claim** — pattern of exceeding all competitors (including Chinese peer Vector) suggests measurement methodology differs from Western norm. Needs verification.
- **Optical quality** — no public reviews of Ventus 1-10× Jupiter-series glass. Tracking, CA, edge sharpness all unknown.
- **No public warranty or MTBF data.**

### Realistic competitive peer

**Vector Optics Continental x10 1-10×28 FFP (SCFF-47, ~$750–849)** is Ventus's realistic retail peer. Against Vector:
- Vector wins on elevation (51 MIL vs 34.9 MIL) and click value (0.1 MIL vs 1/2 MOA).
- Ventus wins on weight (527g / 553g 30mm-tube vs Vector's 651g 34mm-only).
- Ventus offers the 30mm-tube option; Vector does not.
- Vector is currently available at retail; Ventus is pre-retail/OEM.

Against Western FFP 1-10× ($1,000–$2,000 tier): Vortex Razor Gen III, Athlon Ares ETR, Trijicon Credo HX all lose to Ventus on elevation and (for the 30mm SKUs) weight. They win on turret precision (0.1 MIL/0.25 MOA clicks), glass reputation, and resale/warranty.

Against Primary Arms: PA GLx 1-10×24 ($680) is a 30mm-tube FFP 1-10× direct competitor — lighter-objective (24mm), more elevation (~40 MIL), finer click (0.1 MIL). This is the most difficult head-to-head in the class: GLx wins on turret spec, Ventus wins on weight and FOV claim.
