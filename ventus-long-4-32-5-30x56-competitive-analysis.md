# Ventus Long-Range 4-32× / 5-25× / 5-30× × 56mm Class (34mm Tube) — Competitive Analysis

**Class:** Premium long-range FFP, 4-32× / 5-25× / 5-30× magnification, 56mm objective, **34mm tube**
**Ventus SKUs in class:** 3 (FFP 4-32×56, FFP 5-25×56, FFP 5-30×56)

> **Tube-strict comparison.** Ventus is 34mm in this class. ZCO ZC527 uses a **36mm tube** — physically 2mm larger, structurally different erector internal geometry — and is excluded from direct comparison. Only 34mm-tube alpha-tier competitors appear below.

---

## 1. TL;DR — Main Findings

1. **Ventus 46.5 MIL elevation leads the 34mm alpha-tier by 10+ MIL.** Beats Vortex Razor HD Gen III (36.1 MRAD), Nightforce ATACR 5-25 F1 (35 MRAD), Tangent Theta TT525P (30 MRAD), Schmidt & Bender PM II 5-25 (27 MRAD), March-FX 5-40 Gen II (22–24 MIL).
2. **Weight 880g is class-leading** — 175–400g lighter than all European/American alpha-tier (NF 1066g, S&B 1065g, TT 1150g, Vortex Razor Gen III 1278g). Only Vector Continental (810g) is lighter.
3. **Length 375mm is class-shortest.** Vortex Razor Gen III 387mm, NF ATACR 391mm, Vector Continental 393mm, March-FX 389mm, S&B 420mm, TT 425mm.
4. **Windage 23.3 MIL ties Nightforce ATACR exactly** (23 MRAD) as class-leading. Beats ZCO (which is excluded on tube grounds anyway), TT (20 MRAD), Vortex Razor III (15.5 MRAD), S&B (12 MRAD), March (11.5 MIL).
5. **Eye relief 91mm** — standard alpha-tier (S&B, TT, NF all ~89–90mm).
6. **Price class separation is stark.** Ventus and Vector Continental at $700–900; everyone else $3,200–$6,500. Realistic retail peer is Vector Continental; alpha-tier is value-framed positioning.

## 6. Competitive Position Summary

### Where Ventus wins (on paper, 34mm-strict)

- **Elevation** — 46.5 MIL leads class by 10+ MIL vs every alpha-tier peer.
- **Windage** — 23.3 MIL ties NF ATACR as class-leading.
- **Weight** — 880g is 175–400g lighter than all European/American premium.
- **Length** — 375mm is class-shortest.
- **Dual click option** — 0.1 MIL or 1/4 MOA.
- **4× low-end zoom (4-32× SKU)** — unique in class.
- **Price class** — OEM sub-$1000 vs $3,200–$6,500 alpha-tier.

### Where Ventus has risk / uncertainty

- **46.5 MIL elevation on a 34mm tube** — at or beyond the physical limit for conventional erector architecture. Plausible for deliberately-engineered ELR-focused design but **strongly recommend independent tracking verification** on production units before marketing against NF/S&B/TT alpha-tier.
- **Optical quality / reticle quality / coatings** — invisible from specs. This is what $3,000–$6,000 premium pricing actually buys — glass pedigree, chromatic aberration control, edge-to-edge sharpness.
- **Tracking / return-to-zero / repeatability under load** — where alpha-tier mechanical reliability earns its premium. Ventus has no public review record or long-term data.
- **FOV figures** are manufacturer-published, not independently measured.
- **No published MTBF or warranty data.**

### Realistic retail peer

**Vector Optics Continental x6 5-30×56 FFP (SCFF-30, ~$674–833)** is the direct retail peer on 34mm. Against Vector:
- Vector wins on weight (810g vs 880g) and established track record.
- Ventus wins on elevation (46.5 vs 30 MIL), windage (23.3 vs 16 MIL), length (375 vs 393mm).

**Against alpha-tier ($3,000–$6,500)** — Ventus is not a direct on-merit competitor to NF ATACR / S&B PM II / March-FX / TT525P / Vortex Razor Gen III. Positioning argument:

> "100%+ of the travel (if the 46.5 MIL holds up in tracking), 70% of the weight, 90% of the length, at 15–25% of the price. The remaining optical-quality and mechanical-repeatability delta is where the $3,000–$5,000 premium lives."

For a reseller (RikaNV), retail positioning should be meaningfully above Vector Continental ($700–800) but well below Arken/Athlon Cronus/Burris XTR III. Target street: $800–$1,300. At that price, Ventus dominates its tier on paper spec and is a clear choice for ELR-leaning buyers who prioritize elevation over brand pedigree.
