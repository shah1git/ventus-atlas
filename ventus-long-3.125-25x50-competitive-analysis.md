# Ventus Long-Range 3.125-25× / 5-25× × 50mm Class — Competitive Analysis

**Class:** Long-range FFP/SFP, 3.125-25× / 5-25× magnification, 50mm objective, 30mm tube
**Ventus SKUs in class:** 4 (FFP 3.125-25×50, FFP 5-25×50, SFP 3.125-25×50, SFP 5-25×50)

---

## 1. TL;DR — Main Findings

1. **Elevation travel (32.0 MIL) on a 30mm tube matches Nightforce NX8 2.5-20×50 F1 exactly** — and beats every other 30mm-tube 5-25× class FFP. Vortex Viper PST Gen II 5-25×50 (30mm) offers only 20 MIL; Athlon Midas TAC 6-24×50 (30mm) offers 25 MIL. Even 34mm premium (S&B PM II 5-25×56 at 27 MRAD, Burris XTR III 5.5-30×56 at 26 MIL) are exceeded.
2. **Windage travel 26.2 MIL is class-leading.** Higher than Nightforce NX8 (23 MRAD), S&B PM II (±6 MRAD working), Burris XTR III (16 MIL), Vortex Viper PST (10 MIL).
3. **Structurally unusual zoom range.** The 3.125-25× zoom (Jupiter) gives an 8× zoom ratio with a 3.125× low-end on a 30mm tube — no Western brand offers this combination. The 5-25× (Venus) is a standard 5× zoom but still at 30mm, which is rare.
4. **Ventus's realistic peer list is thin.** The only 30mm-tube 5-25× FFP alternatives are Vortex Viper PST Gen II, Athlon Midas TAC, and Nightforce NX8. Ventus fits between Vortex/Athlon (on spec, better) and Nightforce (on spec, tied; on price, far cheaper).
5. **Weight 765g is class-leading** for 30+ MIL elevation in long-range FFP. Only Nightforce NX8 (802g) and Athlon Midas TAC (745g) come close.
6. **Against the alpha-tier (S&B PM II 5-25×56, ZCO, Nightforce ATACR),** Ventus's 34mm-tube 5-25 sibling (see the 4-32/5-25/5-30×56 class file) is the more relevant comparator. This 30mm-tube 5-25 is targeted at the mid-price tier.

## 6. Competitive Position Summary

### Where Ventus wins (on paper)

- **Elevation on 30mm tube** — tied with Nightforce NX8 at 32 MIL, which is the best 30mm-tube result in this class.
- **Windage travel** — 26.2 MIL is class-leading.
- **Weight** — 765g is lightest FFP with full elevation in class.
- **3.125× low-end zoom** — only Nightforce NX8 (2.5×) starts lower, and NX8 caps at 20×.
- **Dual click option** — 0.1 MIL or 1/4 MOA.
- **Price class** — sub-$1000 OEM vs $2,250+ (NF NX8) / $1,800+ (Burris) / $3,200+ (S&B).

### Where Ventus has risk / uncertainty

- **Tube diameter** — 30mm at this magnification range is uncommon. PRS/ELR buyers may favor 34mm by default.
- **32 MIL on 30mm is at the physical ceiling** — plausible but worth independent tracking verification (same caveat as the 3-24×50 class).
- **8× zoom (3.125-25×) at 765g and 349mm** — aggressive for a 30mm tube. Optical performance at the extremes (3.125× low-end and 25× high-end) needs hands-on verification.
- **Optical quality** — no public reviews. Glass CA, edge sharpness, tracking repeatability all unknown.
- **No published MTBF or warranty data.**

### Realistic competitive peer

**Athlon Midas TAC 6-24×50 FFP (~$687–700)** is the direct 30mm-tube price-peer.
- Ventus wins on elevation (32 vs 25 MIL), windage (26.2 vs 25 MIL), and low-end magnification (3.125× vs 6×).
- Midas TAC wins on glass reputation and proven tracking.

**Vortex Viper PST Gen II 5-25×50 FFP (~$999–1,099)** is the direct 30mm/50mm tube-obj match.
- Ventus wins on elevation (32 vs 20 MIL) and windage (26.2 vs 10 MIL).
- Vortex wins on US warranty (VIP) and glass reputation.

**Nightforce NX8 2.5-20×50 F1 ($2,250–2,600)** is the tied-on-elevation premium peer.
- Ventus wins on windage (26.2 vs 23 MRAD), lower weight (765g vs 802g), and price (~3× cheaper).
- NF wins on tracking proven, optical pedigree, US service, and 2.5× low-end (vs Ventus's 3.125× or 5× low-end).

Against 34mm alpha-tier (S&B PM II 5-25×56 at $3,200+): Ventus wins on elevation (32 vs 27 MIL), windage (26.2 vs 12 MIL working), weight (765 vs 1065g). S&B wins on everything that matters for the $3,000+ premium spend: glass, mechanical reliability, serviceability.
